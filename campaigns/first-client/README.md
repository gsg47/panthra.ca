# PANTHRA — First Client Email Campaign

Cold outreach playbook to book your first paying engagement. Built for Calgary / Alberta SMBs that need enterprise-grade security without an in-house SOC.

## Goal

**Book 5 discovery calls → close 1 paid engagement** (Security Assessment or Vigil starter package).

Do not optimize for opens or vanity replies. Optimize for booked 20-minute calls.

## Ideal Customer Profile (ICP)

| Attribute | Target |
|-----------|--------|
| Company size | 15–150 employees |
| Location | Calgary first, then Alberta, then Western Canada |
| Decision maker | Owner, Managing Partner, Office Manager, IT Manager, Operations Manager |
| Tech posture | Microsoft 365 / Google Workspace; no dedicated security team |
| Pain | Worried about phishing, ransomware, client data, cyber insurance, PIPEDA / industry rules |
| Budget signal | Pays for IT support, accounting software, or cyber insurance |

### Best-fit industries (in order)

1. **Legal & professional services** — privilege + client trust; Law Society expectations
2. **Healthcare / clinics** — HIA / PIPA / patient records
3. **Financial / accounting / insurance brokers** — client financial data + PIPEDA
4. **Professional services / agencies** — client IP, email compromise risk
5. **Education / private schools / training orgs** — FOIP / student data

Skip large enterprises and companies that already have a full security team for this first campaign.

## The Offer (lead magnet)

Keep the ask tiny:

> **Complimentary 30-minute Security Snapshot** — a live walkthrough of your Microsoft 365 / email / identity posture, with a one-page risk summary afterward. No pitch deck. No obligation.

Why this works for a first client:
- Low commitment for them
- Lets you show competence fast
- Natural upsell into a paid assessment or Vigil tier

## Positioning (one line)

> PANTHRA gives Calgary businesses silent, practical cybersecurity — MFA, email protection, monitoring, and response — without enterprise pricing or jargon.

Tagline on site: **Silent Protection. Absolute Security.**

## Campaign math (realistic)

| Step | Target |
|------|--------|
| Personalized emails sent | 40–60 |
| Positive replies | 4–8 |
| Calls booked | 3–5 |
| Paid engagements closed | 1 |

Quality beats volume. 10 highly researched emails beat 200 generic blasts.

## How to run this (weekly cadence)

### Week setup (once)

1. Confirm sending from a real domain mailbox (`contact@panthra.ca` or your name `@panthra.ca`).
2. Warm the domain: send normal mail for a few days before cold outreach.
3. Add a short, human signature (name, title, panthra.ca, Calgary).
4. Import `prospects/tracker.csv` into Google Sheets and set up dropdowns (see `prospects/tracker-guide.md`).

### Daily loop (45–90 minutes)

1. Research **5–8** prospects (see `prospects/research-checklist.md`).
2. Send **5–8** Email 1 messages (personalized first line required).
3. Process replies same day (`emails/reply-scripts.md`).
4. Log everything in the tracker — update `status`, dates, and `next_follow_up`.

### Follow-up schedule

| Email | When |
|-------|------|
| Email 1 — Snapshot offer | Day 0 |
| Email 2 — Specific risk | Day 3 |
| Email 3 — Social proof / why us | Day 7 |
| Email 4 — Soft break-up | Day 12 |

Stop the sequence the moment they reply (yes, no, or question).

## Compliance & reputation rules

- Only contact **business** addresses you can justify (public website, LinkedIn, referral).
- One personalized thread per company — no BCC blasts.
- Include a clear opt-out line in Email 1 and Email 4.
- Do not buy scraped lists or use automated mass-mail tools for this first campaign.
- CASL (Canada): B2B cold email is allowed when relevant to the recipient’s role/business, but keep volume low, identify yourself clearly, and honour opt-outs immediately.

## After the first client

1. Ask for a short testimonial and referral intros.
2. Turn the engagement into a 3–4 sentence case study (problem → action → result).
3. Reuse that proof in Email 3 for the next 50 prospects.
