# Industry variants

Swap the `{{Hook}}` block and lightly tune Email 2’s scenario. Keep the same CTAs as `sequence-core.md`.

---

## Clinics / health-adjacent

**Hook ideas:**

- New patient portal / online booking mentions on the site
- Hiring front-desk or medical office assistants
- PIPEDA / Alberta health privacy questions from patients or partners

**Email 2 scenario swap:**

```
A pattern we keep seeing with clinics: a fake “patient records request” or supplier invoice hits the front desk, and PHI / billing access is the real risk — not just a locked laptop.
```

**Language to prefer:** privacy, patient trust, continuity of care  
**Avoid:** fear language about “hospital breaches” unless it’s a real, cited local story.

---

## Energy services / field operations

**Hook ideas:**

- Field supervisors / remote crews on Microsoft 365 or shared drives
- Vendor portals, ticket systems, or OT-adjacent tooling mentioned publicly
- Growth / new yard / new region announcements

**Email 2 scenario swap:**

```
A pattern we keep seeing with field-heavy teams: a believable “dispatch / timesheet / invoice” email reaches a supervisor on a phone, and the compromise spreads before anyone at the office notices.
```

**Language to prefer:** uptime, crew safety of systems, vendor access  
**Avoid:** claiming OT/ICS expertise you haven’t scoped.

---

## Professional services (legal, accounting, consulting)

**Hook ideas:**

- Client confidentiality / trust language on the site
- Seasonal volume (tax season, deal flow)
- Insurance or client security questionnaires

**Email 2 scenario swap:**

```
A pattern we keep seeing with professional firms: a wire-change or “client document” email looks routine, and the damage is reputation + client funds — not just IT cleanup.
```

**Language to prefer:** client trust, matter / engagement confidentiality, insurance readiness

---

## Construction / trades

**Hook ideas:**

- Multiple job sites, shared mailboxes, subcontractors
- Recent project wins posted publicly
- Bookkeeper / project coordinator as the real inbox power user

**Email 2 scenario swap:**

```
A pattern we keep seeing with trades: a fake supplier or “change order” email hits the person who pays invoices, and the money is gone before the GC or owner hears about it.
```

**Language to prefer:** invoice fraud, job-site access, simple controls that busy teams will actually use
