# Industry Opening Hooks

Use these as the **personalization sentence** in Email 1, and optionally tweak Email 2's risk list. Keep the rest of the sequence the same.

---

## Legal & professional services

```
Firms like {{Company}} are frequent phishing targets because a single compromised mailbox can expose privileged client files overnight.
```

Email 2 risk angle: business email compromise impersonating a partner or trust account instructions.

CTA framing: "protect privilege and client trust."

---

## Healthcare / clinics

```
Clinics like {{Company}} sit on patient records and Microsoft 365 — ransomware and account takeover tend to hit hardest when staff are focused on care, not IT.
```

Email 2 risk angle: ransomware downtime + unauthorized access to records (HIA / PIPA / PIPEDA).

CTA framing: "keep care running and patient data private."

---

## Financial / accounting / insurance brokers

```
{{Company}} handles sensitive financial data daily — attackers know one successful phishing email can lead to wire fraud or account takeover.
```

Email 2 risk angle: invoice fraud, client portal credential theft, PIPEDA exposure.

CTA framing: "reduce fraud and client data risk without slowing operations."

---

## Professional services / agencies

```
Agencies like {{Company}} live in email and shared drives — a compromised login usually means client IP and conversations leave quietly.
```

Email 2 risk angle: vendor account compromise, shared mailbox gaps, weak MFA.

CTA framing: "protect client work and your reputation."

---

## Education / training organizations

```
Schools and training orgs like {{Company}} are soft targets for phishing because staff and students share a lot of accounts and file access.
```

Email 2 risk angle: Microsoft 365 account takeover, ransomware on shared drives (FOIP / PIPA).

CTA framing: "keep learning online and student data protected."

---

## Manufacturing / OT-light SMBs

```
Manufacturers your size often harden the plant floor but leave office email and remote access soft — that's usually how ransomware gets in.
```

Email 2 risk angle: phishing → IT network → production downtime.

CTA framing: "stop ransomware before it reaches operations."
